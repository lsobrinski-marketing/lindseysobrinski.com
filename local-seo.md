## For appointment-driven retail, the KPI is bookings — not "near me"

Local SEO gets measured on the wrong thing. Ranking for "near me" is table stakes; it gets a business found. It says nothing about whether the page communicates what the location actually offers, or whether the visit turns into a booking. For multi-location and appointment-driven retail, the honest scoreboard is booked appointments and revenue per location.

And the fastest way to move that number is usually not new content. It's exposing what's already true.

## The wins hiding in plain sight

**Trust signals you already earned but never exposed.** On one location, more than 7,000 reviews averaging well above four stars were completely invisible in search — no review schema, so the rating never reached the engine. That's a trust signal a business spent years earning and then hid by accident. Exposing it takes no new reviews and moves conversion immediately.

**Services that read as a generic store.** A location can rank locally and still book a fraction of its goal because its consultations and specialist services aren't marked up. To the engine, a page offering high-value, high-intent services looks like any other store. Service schema is often the difference between being found for what you do and not existing for it.

**Pages that describe, but don't convert.** Generic, templated store copy ranks but doesn't sell the visit. The location's real differentiators — services, specialists, the experience — belong on the page, in structured form, aimed at the booking.

> The best local trust signal for one retailer was a database field it never exposed to search: 7,000+ reviews the engine couldn't see. The highest-return local work is often making what already exists visible to search, not manufacturing something new.

## How I work this lane — and what's coming

The sequence is: expose the data you already own (reviews, services, attributes) with the right structured data, make each location page convert the visit rather than just rank for it, then judge the whole program on booked appointments and revenue per location.
